**1. Provide a detailed explanation of your submission.**

Robyn is an autonomous back office for freelancers. It connects a real Google Calendar, a real Gmail inbox and a real Xero org, and closes the gap between work that happened and work that got invoiced.

The core idea is that the evidence of unbilled work already exists. Meetings sit in the calendar, agreed scope sits in call transcripts, and new client relationships start in the inbox. Robyn runs three loops over that evidence. First, every synced meeting is matched to a client by attendee email and priced from the contract on file. Second, when a transcript is pasted in, Claude parses it into strict JSON and a deterministic pricing engine catches billable work mentioned in conversation that never landed on an invoice, prices it from the contract's own billing rules (base rate, reduced rate tiers, 30 minute minimum blocks, payment terms) and cites the exact clause. Third, unknown meeting attendees are queued as potential clients, Robyn watches the inbox for exactly those senders and nobody else, and when a signed agreement PDF arrives it creates the client, files the contract and creates the Xero contact with zero forms.

The architectural rule we never broke: the LLM parses and proposes, it never decides and never sends. Pricing and autonomy decisions live in a pure TypeScript engine with 79 unit tests and zero model calls. A policy gate checks five conditions (autonomy on, contract on file, exact client match, amount within terms, every line priced from the contract) and either writes to Xero or raises a review task. There is no silent third path, and every verdict, approval and Xero write creates an audit event.

We proved the whole thing live before submitting. A real meeting synced in, its transcript revealed 15 minutes of uninvoiced review work, and with zero clicks Robyn priced it as one 30 minute minimum block under Clause 3, wrote INV-0073 to Xero as AUTHORISED with its decision note in History and the evidence attached, and asked Xero to email it to the client. Minutes later a prospect emailed a signed agreement PDF and Robyn onboarded them end to end: 12 clauses parsed, billing profile extracted, Xero contact created, all on the audit trail.

Process wise, two coding agents built this side by side. Claude Code owned the NestJS backend, the engine and the integrations, Cursor owned the Next.js frontend, and the committed OpenAPI contract was the only interface between them. Frontend types are generated from the spec and never hand written.

**2. How did your project utilize the Xero API?**

The core workflow is automated invoicing with full autonomy under a policy gate. When the engine prices billable work and the policy passes, Robyn ensures the Xero contact, creates the ACCREC invoice idempotently (check by reference before create), moves it to AUTHORISED, writes its decision note into the invoice History, attaches the transcript excerpt and contract clause as evidence Attachments, and has Xero email the invoice to the client. Around that, Robyn reads Payments to spot broken retainer cadences, Quotes to compare accepted work against invoiced work, and Aged Receivables to power the "money walking away" strip on the dashboard. All access is rate limit aware with caching, hard timeouts and idempotent writes.

**3. Which specific Xero API endpoints did your application interact with?**

- POST https://identity.xero.com/connect/token (OAuth 2.0 client credentials for the Custom Connection)
- GET /connections and GET /Organisation (live health probe)
- GET /Contacts, POST /Contacts (find by email or name, idempotent create on onboarding)
- GET /Invoices, GET /Invoices/{id}, POST /Invoices (check by reference, create ACCREC, authorise)
- POST /Invoices/{id}/Email (Xero emails the authorised invoice to the client)
- GET and PUT /Invoices/{id}/History (decision notes on the invoice record)
- GET and PUT /Invoices/{id}/Attachments/{filename} (evidence attachments)
- GET /Payments, PUT /Payments
- GET /Quotes
- GET /Accounts (default sales account resolution)
- GET /Reports/AgedReceivablesByContact
