Technical Advisory Agreement

Advisory services for a civic technology platform

This Technical Advisory Agreement (the "Agreement") is made on 12 January 2026 between:

CivicPulse-DevanshKaria, a civic technology company operating a resident engagement and council consultation platform (the "Client"); and

Devansh Karia, an independent technology consultant (the "Consultant").

1. Engagement

  1. 1.1The Client engages the Consultant to provide ongoing technical advisory services, and the Consultant accepts the engagement on the terms of this Agreement.
  2. 1.2The engagement is non-exclusive. The Consultant may provide services to other clients provided no conflict of interest arises under clause 6.

2. Services

  1. 2.1The Consultant shall advise the Client on the architecture, scalability and engineering practices of the Client's civic engagement platform, including its consultation workflows, resident notification services, reporting pipelines and public sector integrations.
  2. 2.2Advisory services are delivered primarily through scheduled advisory sessions held by video call or in person.
  3. 2.3Code reviews of the Client's repositories carried out by the Consultant, and preparation carried out at the Client's request, count as billable advisory time under clause 3.

3. Fees

  1. 3.1Advisory time is charged per hour.
  2. 3.2Where the total advisory time in a billing week is three (3) hours or fewer, the rate is £50 (fifty pounds) per hour.
  3. 3.3Where the total advisory time in a billing week exceeds three (3) hours, a rate of £40 (forty pounds) per hour applies to the whole of that week's advisory time.
  4. 3.4The minimum billing block is thirty (30) minutes. Any advisory session, code review or requested preparation bills at least one block.
  5. 3.5Time beyond the first block rounds up to the nearest thirty (30) minutes.
  6. 3.6All fees are exclusive of VAT, which is added where applicable.

4. Invoicing and Payment

  1. 4.1The Consultant invoices weekly, with each invoice covering the advisory time of the preceding week.
  2. 4.2Invoices are payable within seven (7) days of the invoice date.
  3. 4.3The Consultant may charge statutory interest on sums overdue under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998.

5. Independent Contractor

  1. 5.1The Consultant is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates a relationship of employment, partnership or agency between the parties.
  2. 5.2The Consultant is responsible for the Consultant's own tax and National Insurance contributions.

6. Confidentiality

  1. 6.1Each party shall keep confidential all non-public information received from the other in connection with this Agreement, and shall use it only for the purposes of the engagement.
  2. 6.2This obligation survives termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

7. Intellectual Property

  1. 7.1All intellectual property in the Client's platform and repositories remains the property of the Client.
  2. 7.2Any written advice, review notes or documentation produced by the Consultant specifically for the Client under this Agreement is assigned to the Client on payment of the corresponding invoice.

8. Term and Termination

  1. 8.1This Agreement commences on the date first written above and continues until terminated by either party on fourteen (14) days' written notice.
  2. 8.2Termination does not affect the Client's obligation to pay for advisory time already provided.

9. Liability

  1. 9.1The Consultant provides advice in good faith; decisions on implementation remain the Client's own. The Consultant's aggregate liability under this Agreement is limited to the fees paid in the three (3) months preceding the claim.
  2. 9.2Nothing in this Agreement limits liability for fraud or for death or personal injury caused by negligence.

10. Governing Law

  1. 10.1This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales, and the parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.
